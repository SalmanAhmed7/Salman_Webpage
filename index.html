About Me

Hello there! I am Salman. And I am a programmer. Besides programming, I like to play soccer and listen to American pop rock, namely OneRepublic. I also enjoy going fishing with my dad most Saturdays. If not fishing, you can expect to see me playing soccer in the local park with friends. I guess I am both funny and serious from time to time. I am serious when I am programming at work. I am funny when I am programming for personal reasons.
Did I mention how much I love soccer? Initially, I wanted to become a soccer player. However, all of this changed when I met Java for the first time in 2015. I enjoyed programming with Java sooo much that I could't think about anything else. I guess I was drinking too much Java, the Arabica coffee. Anyways, this programming language started a new journey in my life. Right now, I am simply living it. And it's amazing.
I would not have been a programmer today if I didn't go to Arizona State University. At ASU, while I was studying Mechanical Engineering, I saw a friend of mine writing some pieces of code to automate a list of tasks. For me, this was like magic. I wanted to automate my tasks too. And my friend was kind enough to help me. And this was it. I decided that I wanted to become a programmer. Next morning, I decided to switch my major.

My Experiences

I am currently a Software Engineer at Collins Aerospace. I started working here since 2019. Some of my duties include, but not limited to, updating Flight-critical customer documents, retrieving and formatting legacy software data so that old customer requirements are compatible with new software tools, and performing code and script reviews on the different components of the Boeing 737-700 AEWC Wedgetail.
I was an Embedded Software Engineering Intern at UTC Aerospace Systems. I worked here as an intern for 11 months before converting to a full-time employee. During my internship, I worked on a N2 Imaging Systems' power management tool of a microcontroller that would power up a weapon reticle system (M2WS). I worked on fixing, as well as writing new requirements for, the indicator codes of the menu system of the weapon device.
Before my graduation, I participated in many college events like Hackathons and Cyber Security Challenges. PayPal Opportunity Hack of 2017 was one of the most funnest. Amazing food and amazing competing environment. For 36 hours straight, me and my teammates coded using JavaScript and SQL to develop a website for our client, a non-profit organization. After this day, I started to live by this philosophy: "Eat less, Code more."
